  • Schroeder Laboratory Intern (August 2019 - January 2020)

    In this project, we aimed to select, grow, and screen mutant candidates of Arabidopsis thaliana that exhibit the "hot" phenotype. We began by autoclaving the soil and sterilizing the seeds to ensure a controlled environment. Seeds were initially sown in a petri dish, and once mature seedlings developed, they were carefully transplanted into pots for further growth. To assess the phenotypic variations, we analyzed and compared the stomata and pavement cells of both stagnant and mutant plants using infrared cameras.   • Fricovsky Laboratory Intern (June-August 2018)

    The process of glycolysis provides a continuous energy source for cancer cells, fueling their growth and proliferation. The aim of this study was to identify an inhibitor that could effectively disrupt glycolysis, thereby stifling the growth of cancer cells by targeting the key regulatory enzyme, phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1). Sulforaphane, a naturally occurring compound found in cruciferous vegetables like broccoli, cabbage, and kale, was investigated for its potential as an inhibitor. As an organic isothiocyanate, sulforaphane has been documented to inhibit PFK-1 activity, which is crucial for energy production in cancer cells. Our results demonstrated that 0.15 mM sulforaphane effectively inhibited PFK-1 activity by 50%, providing promising evidence for its role in potentially controlling glycolysis and, consequently, cancer cell growth.

  • Matcha Watch

    Recently, Marukyu Koyamaen Matcha has gone viral on TikTok and has sold out on every single restock since June. In order to get to the restocks on time, I created this stock tracker of their principal matchas. In order to create this tracker, I webscraped the website using BeautifulSoup 4/ python. This data is stored in an AWS DynamoDB database and is regularly updated through a scheduled EventBridge trigger, which invokes a Lambda function. The stock status is then made accessible via a REST API exposed through API Gateway, which calls another Lambda function to query the database. The front end of the tracker is built using HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap 4, and JavaScript fetches the stock data from the API and displays it. Additionally, when any stock status is detected to have changed from out of stock to in stock, a discord bot is triggered to ping a notification in a designated discord channel with the product ID and URL.
